Background

I was born in Israel, and lived in Rehovot with my parents for the first few years of my childhood. At the age of five, my father had to be relocated to California for work, and so I moved for the first time. Initially, going to elementary school was tough, as I was unfamiliar with American culture, policies, and only knew Hebrew. However, with time I quickly resolved these issues. By the end of fifth grade, I had plenty of friends, knew English better than Hebrew, and graduated from elementary school. However, at the same time, my dad's work relocated him again. Thus, my family moved from California to Massachusetts, and I once again had to reengage in the culture and the community. This time however, I also had to learn to adapt to the weather, as the first time I saw snow was in Massachusetts. It was around this time that I also first developed an interest in computer science. In 8th grade, I took a course on making games with Scratch, and the same summer I took a summer course in basic C++. However, it was in 11th grade when I really became engrossed in computer science. I took a semester long course on C++, and in 12th grade took AP Computer Science, an advanced year-long Java course. By the end of high school, I decided to pursue a degree in computer science, and am now doing so at Reichman University.
